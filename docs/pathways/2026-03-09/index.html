Oxidative Stress and the Antioxidant Defense System👇 ✅Cells constantly produce reactive oxygen species (ROS) during normal metabolism. Major ROS molecules include superoxide (O₂⁻), hydrogen peroxide (H₂O₂), and the hydroxyl radical (OH•). When ROS accumulate beyond normal levels, they can damage important cellular components. ✅Excess ROS can cause DNA damage, lipid peroxidation of cell membranes, and oxidation of proteins, leading to impaired cellular function and contributing to the development of many diseases. ✅To counteract these harmful effects, cells rely on a complex antioxidant defense system. Key components include the glutathione (GSH) system, thioredoxin (TRX) system, and antioxidant enzymes such as superoxide dismutase (SOD) and catalase (CAT). ✅Additional protection is provided by transition metal binding proteins and diet-derived antioxidants such as vitamins C and E. Together, these systems maintain the balance between ROS production and detoxification, protecting cells from oxidative damage. 💡 Selvaraj, N.R.; Nandan, D.; Nair, B.G.; Nair, V.A.; Venugopal, P.; Aradhya, R.
