DNA Damage and Repair Pathways👇
✅Cells are constantly exposed to endogenous and environmental factors that can damage DNA. These insults can cause strand breaks, base modifications, crosslinks, or mutations. To maintain genomic stability, cells activate specialized DNA repair pathways depending on the type of damage.
✅Single-strand breaks (SSBs) are primarily repaired through the Base Excision Repair (BER) pathway. Proteins such as PARP1 detect the damage, and enzymes like APE1, XRCC1, and DNA polymerase β restore the correct DNA sequence.
✅More severe damage occurs with double-strand breaks (DSBs). These lesions are repaired either by Non-Homologous End Joining (NHEJ), a rapid but error-prone pathway, or by Homologous Recombination (HR), a high-fidelity repair mechanism that uses a homologous DNA template.
✅DNA lesions such as bulky adducts, crosslinks, and oxidized bases are corrected through Nucleotide Excision Repair (NER). This pathway removes the damaged DNA segment and replaces it with newly synthesized DNA.
✅Finally, Mismatch Repair (MMR) corrects replication errors such as base mismatches or small insertions and deletions, ensuring high fidelity during DNA replication.
